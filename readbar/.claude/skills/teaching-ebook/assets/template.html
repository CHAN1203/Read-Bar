Chapter 1 · The example

1.1 How rain forms

What it is

Rain is liquid water that falls when tiny droplets in a cloud merge until they are too heavy for the rising air to hold.

The logic

The sun heats the surface, water evaporates and rises, cools as it climbs, and condenses onto dust into droplets. Droplets collide and grow; once gravity beats the updraft, they fall.

Think about it
If warm air rises and cools as it climbs, why does it rain more on the windward side of a mountain than the leeward side?

Air forced up the windward slope cools and dumps its moisture as rain. By the time it crests and descends the leeward side it is already dry and warming — so the far side sits in a rain shadow. The mountain didn't remove the water; it just made the air release it on one side first.

Good vs bad signal

Tall, towering clouds with dark bases = strong updrafts and likely rain. Thin, high wisps = ice crystals far from raining.
